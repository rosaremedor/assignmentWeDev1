Tequeños

Chesse Stiks or "Tequeños" are Venezuela’s quintessential finger food. Made by spiraling dough around a stick of queso blanco, it's then fried or baked, and served with a dipping sauce, like guasacaca (similiar to Mexican guacamole). It’s a gooey, satisfying affair you'll want to get involved in. Venezuelan eat this dish as finger food for parties, bithdays and weddings. Also, a large version is sell in schools and universities canteens as a form of breakfats or quick snack to continue until your get your main meal of the day.

Arepas

Arepas are crisp on the outside and fluffy on the inside. They are basically cornmeal cakes made from pre-cooked cornmeal that are grilled, baked, or fried. Historically, arepas were cooked on a “budare”, a smooth, flat stone on which the arepa is cooked. Once the arepa is cooked, they are split in the center and then filled with beans, cheese or your favorite filling. Because Venezuelan are from have Latin roots and are in the Caribbean, there are names for the favorites fillings, such as "Dotted Queen"(a mixture of ripe avocado, chicken and grated cheese), The Picky (pulled beef with grated cheese), The Teenager (Ham and Grated chesse), among others. You can see the menu and order some from this Venezuelan restaurant Arepas Grill if you live in Dublin South.
